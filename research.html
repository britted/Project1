Brain Computer Interface

In a class, we all worked indivually (with collaboration when necessary) to utilize various computer programs and procedures to create Brain Computer Interface (BCI) algorithms using EEG signals. Accoustic source location, data acquisition, signal processing, and BCI data anylsis were the principles at the foundation of this project. First, using LabView, the location of the accoustic source was found, then the location of the source with respect to the robots were determined, and then the robots were programmed to rotate and move the the source. Then in Matlab, the EEG data was collected, with the Emotiv headset, and analyzed to create distinct movement profiles for each defined movement - the movements of interest were moving the head left and right. These movement profiles were then incorporated to the LabView code to control the movements of the robot.

Machine Learning - University of Queensland

In class, experimented with various machine learning techniques and algorithms to learn which would be optimal in certain situations. As a final research project, did a case study that aimed to reproduce the results found in the study by David Arthur and Sergei Vassilvitskii, which analyzes the efficiency of the k means clustering algorithms based on different values of the variable k. The techniques used were various implementations of the k means and k means++ algorithms. The k means++ algorithm is a combined algorithm based on the fundamental principles of the k means algorithm, but differs in its initialization. The correctness off each these models were analyzed after implementation. This study attempts to recreate the results found by Arthur and Vassilvitskii. by implementing the three algorithms and classifying the models produced. Notwithstanding the multiple factors that hinder the precision of the reproduction of the results, the overall results were comparable to what were found by Arthur and Vassilvitskii.

Biofuels

Subsequent to an International Experience trip to Brazil, I was responsible for conducting a reserach project that analyzed the renewable energy sources ethanol biofuel produced from sugarcane and photovoltaic cells with a focus on the environmental and economic advantages and disadvantages of each. Brief descriptions of the production and resources needed to produce each were included in my final report. The economic and environmental effects were the main elements of comparison. My final conclusion that photovoltaic cells offered a more global solution, however neither could sustain life on its own - a combination of renewable energy sources would be the optimal solution.
