Systems Lab

In systems lab, I worked with one other partner to derive and apply different equations to work with the system and implemented them using Simulink and Matlab. Using the principles learned in control systems, different types of controllers were used, PD, PID, etc. These different controllers had different effects on the the system and a combination of them were used to allow the ball and beam to properly come to a dyanmic equilibrium. In the end, the formulas derived and the constant values found helped make the ball and beam system balance the ball and allowed it to move the ball to a desried location and hold it there. See video for more info.

Robotics Lab

Using Simulink and Matlab, I worked with three other parters to control a two link robot system. The project was broken up into multiple parts that all added up to the final result - allowing the robot to control the second link by only manipulating the first link.Kinematics and energy principles were the key elements used in this project. The second link, or arm, of the robot needed to stand vertically upwards and was controlled by moving the first link of the robot. The location, or state, of the second arm was always recorded and that state determined the actions of the first arm. When the project was completed, the second arm was able to stay vertically upward as long as the program was running, and would even snap back into this position when preturbed by any force.

Senior Design

Working alongside two other electrical engineering seniors to design a knee stretch monitor for use by postoperative knee surgery patients and those suffering from knee or knee-related injuries. The goal of this device is to allow the patient to monitor his or her knee stretches, as well as to provide feedback to the user through a graphical user interface (GUI) detailing his or her progress, while motivating the patient through alerts to continue stretching in order to avoid more costly surgeries. However, this device is only meant to be used to monitor certain types of knee stretches that primarily consist of measuring the angle of the knee during bending and/or the level of the leg during leg raise exercises. The device will not be used to transmit patient data to third parties, such as the patient's medical provider or physical therapist in order to comply with HIPAA regulations.
 
Disclaimer: This project is currently in progress.

BCI

In a class, we all worked indivually (with collaboration when necessary) to utilize various computer programs and procedures to create Brain Computer Interface (BCI) algorithms using EEG signals. Accoustic source location, data acquisition, signal processing, and BCI data anylsis were the principles at the foundation of this project. First, using LabView, the location of the accoustic source was found, then the location of the source with respect to the robots were determined, and then the robots were programmed to rotate and move the the source. Then in Matlab, the EEG data was collected, with the Emotiv headset, and analyzed to create distinct movement profiles for each defined movement - the movements of interest were moving the head left and right. These movement profiles were then incorporated to the LabView code to control the movements of the robot.

Starbucks in China

As a part of the Fullbridge program, my group of three other member and myself, worked to implement new business strategies based on the company's current and past performace. We analyzed Starbucks and experimented with the idea of creating new stores in China. It was not just about paying to create and open the new store, but we analyzed the different market strategies that would be necessary to have the coffee company be a success in a nation of tea drinkers. We introduced a new product, one that complemented the flavors of the culture and the drinks that already existed and were more suited for the traditional Chinese palette. We presented our project to the program leaders and received feedback to be used in further projects.
